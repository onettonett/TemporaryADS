"One Size Fits None: Expenditure Heterogeneity and the Hidden Inequality in UK Inflation, 2015–2023"

The ONS Consumer Prices Index including owner-occupiers' housing costs (CPIH) is constructed from a single representative basket weighted to the average UK household. This design conceals meaningful variation: households with structurally heavier expenditure shares in food and energy face a materially different price environment than those whose spending is concentrated in more stable categories. Using microdata from the Living Costs and Food Survey, this project constructs household-type-specific COICOP expenditure baskets for nine socioeconomic dimensions — income quintile, tenure, age of reference person, household composition, employment status, disability, and region — and combines these bespoke weights with ONS MM23 item-level CPIH sub-indices under a Laspeyres framework to produce annual group-specific inflation series from 2015/16 to 2023/24. The resulting series are compared against the headline CPIH to quantify how far the average-basket assumption over- or understates inflation for each group. The analysis identifies three distinct inflationary episodes — post-Brexit import price pass-through, COVID-era demand shifts, and the 2021–2023 energy and food shock — with particular focus on crisis-period divergence between groups. Estimates are validated against ONS Household Costs Indices benchmarks.
